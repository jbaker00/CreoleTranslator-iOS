## Description (4000 chars max)

Creole Translator is a free AI-powered speech translation app for Haitian Creole and English. Speak into your phone and get an accurate translation instantly — then hear it read aloud in a natural voice.

**How It Works**
Tap the record button and speak in Haitian Creole or English. The app transcribes your speech using Groq AI, translates it to the other language, and displays both results on screen. Tap the speaker icon to hear either side read aloud.

**Bidirectional Translation**
Switch between Creole → English and English → Creole with a single tap. Perfect for conversations, medical appointments, social services, community outreach, or learning the language.

**Natural AI Voices**
Translations are read aloud using Groq's natural-sounding text-to-speech, so you can hear correct Haitian Creole or English pronunciation — not a robotic computer voice.

**Translation History**
Every translation is saved locally on your device so you can review past conversations at any time. Play back either side of any past translation with the speaker button. Clear your history whenever you like.

**Who Is It For**
• Haitian Creole speakers navigating English-speaking environments
• Healthcare workers, social workers, and teachers serving Haitian communities
• Families communicating across a language barrier
• Travelers and volunteers in Haiti or Haitian diaspora communities
• Anyone learning Haitian Creole

**Privacy**
Your speech is sent to Groq AI for processing over an encrypted connection. Audio is not stored after processing. Translation history stays on your device only and is never shared. See our full privacy policy at https://jbaker00.github.io/CreoleTranslator-iOS/privacy-policy

## App Review Notes

**No account or login required** — the app works immediately on launch.

**Testing the app:**
1. On first launch, a compact privacy notice appears at the bottom of the screen — tap **"Allow & Continue"** to proceed.
2. Grant **Microphone** permission when prompted (required to record speech).
3. The default direction is **Creole → English**. Tap **"Start Recording"**, speak a phrase in Haitian Creole (e.g., "Bonjou, kijan ou rele?"), then tap **"Stop Recording"**.
4. The app will transcribe and translate the audio. Both the Creole and English text appear on screen.
5. Tap the **speaker icon** on either card to hear it read aloud.
6. Tap **"Switch Direction"** to flip to **English → Creole** and try an English phrase.
7. Tap the **clock icon** (top right) to view Translation History. Each entry has a speaker button for both languages.
8. Tap the **gear icon** (top right) to access Settings — voice selection and privacy consent management.

**API Notes for Reviewers:**
The app uses the Groq API for speech transcription, translation, and text-to-speech. API keys are embedded in the build. A network connection is required for all features.

**Ads:**
The app displays a Google AdMob banner at the bottom of the screen. This is expected behavior.

**ATT (App Tracking Transparency):**
The app requests ATT permission (for AdMob personalized ads) after the app becomes active. This is the standard Apple system dialog.

**Permissions used:**
- **Microphone** — required to record speech for translation. Prompted on first use of the record button.
- **App Tracking Transparency** — requested at launch for AdMob personalized ads. Users can decline.
